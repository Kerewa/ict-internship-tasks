. Service Request Management
What It Is

Service Request Management is the practice of handling routine requests from users that do not involve a service failure or disruption. These requests are usually common, predictable, and follow a predefined process. Examples include password resets, software installation, creating email accounts, granting access to systems, or providing new equipment to staff members.

Why It Matters

Many of the requests received by the ICT department are simple and repetitive. Having a structured process for handling them ensures that they are completed quickly and consistently. This improves user satisfaction and allows ICT staff to focus on more complex technical issues instead of spending excessive time on routine tasks. Standardized service requests also make it easier to track progress and ensure that all requests are fulfilled within the expected timeframe.

What Goes Wrong When It Is Missing or Done Poorly

Without a clear service request process, requests may be communicated through informal channels such as phone calls, emails, or verbal conversations, making them difficult to track. Some requests may be forgotten or delayed, while similar requests may be handled differently depending on who receives them. This can lead to frustration among staff, inconsistent service delivery, and unnecessary workload for the ICT team.

AWWDA Example

At AWWDA, a common service request is when a new employee joins the organization. The ICT department needs to create an email account, provide access to relevant systems, assign a computer, and ensure that the employee can perform their duties from the first day. Without a standardized process, these tasks may be overlooked or completed late, causing delays in the employee's work. By using a simple request form and checklist, the ICT department can ensure that every new staff member receives the required accounts, permissions, and equipment in a timely and consistent manner. This improves efficiency and provides a better onboarding experience for new employees.
