Problem Management
What It Is

Problem Management is the practice of identifying and eliminating the root causes of incidents to prevent them from happening again. While Incident Management focuses on restoring services as quickly as possible, Problem Management focuses on investigating why the incident occurred and finding a permanent solution.

Why It Matters

Repeated incidents can consume a significant amount of time and resources if only temporary fixes are applied. Problem Management helps reduce the number of recurring issues by addressing their underlying causes. This improves service reliability, increases productivity, and reduces disruptions to staff. By preventing the same problems from occurring repeatedly, the ICT department can focus on improving services rather than constantly responding to the same incidents.

What Goes Wrong When It Is Missing or Done Poorly

Without Problem Management, the same issues continue to occur because only the symptoms are addressed. For example, a system that crashes regularly may simply be restarted every time it fails without investigating the actual cause. Staff may eventually accept the problem as normal, leading to frustration, reduced efficiency, and increased downtime. In some cases, a small recurring issue may hide a more serious problem that could eventually result in major system failure or data loss.

AWWDA Example

At AWWDA, suppose staff members frequently report that files stored on external backup drives become inaccessible or corrupted. Each time the issue occurs, the ICT department may restore the files from another backup source, allowing work to continue. However, if no one investigates the root cause, the problem will keep recurring. Through Problem Management, the ICT team would analyze the issue and discover whether it is caused by faulty external drives, incorrect backup procedures, outdated drivers, or hardware failure. Once the root cause is identified and corrected, the recurring data access problem is eliminated, reducing future disruptions and ensuring that staff data remains secure and accessible.
