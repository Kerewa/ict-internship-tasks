 Change Enablement
What It Is

Change Enablement is the practice of ensuring that changes made to IT systems, services, or infrastructure are planned, assessed, tested, and approved before implementation. These changes may include software updates, system upgrades, configuration modifications, or the introduction of new technologies. The goal is to enable beneficial changes while minimizing the risk of service disruptions.

Why It Matters

Changes are necessary for improving services, fixing issues, and meeting organizational needs. However, if changes are made without proper planning, they can cause system failures, data loss, or service outages. Change Enablement helps ensure that potential risks are identified in advance, appropriate approvals are obtained, and a recovery plan is available if something goes wrong. This allows the organization to innovate and improve services while maintaining stability.

What Goes Wrong When It Is Missing or Done Poorly

Without a structured change process, staff may make modifications directly to live systems without testing them first. A small mistake in a system configuration or software update can result in downtime, loss of data, or security vulnerabilities. Additionally, when changes are not documented, it becomes difficult to identify the cause of problems when they occur. On the other hand, having too many approval steps for simple changes can slow down operations and reduce efficiency.

AWWDA Example

At AWWDA, the ICT department may decide to implement a new backup solution that automatically stores staff data on external hard drives and secure storage devices to prevent data loss. Before introducing this change, the team should assess the impact, test the backup process, verify that files can be successfully restored, and prepare a rollback plan in case problems arise. If the backup system were introduced without proper testing, important files could fail to back up correctly, putting organizational data at risk. Following a Change Enablement process ensures that the new backup solution is implemented safely and effectively while minimizing disruption to staff operations.
