Incident Management
What It Is

Incident management is the process of restoring normal service operations as quickly as possible whenever a disruption occurs. An incident can be any unexpected interruption or reduction in the quality of an IT service, such as a system outage, network failure, or application error. The main focus of incident management is to minimize the impact on users and ensure that services are returned to normal within the shortest time possible.

Why It Matters

Users often measure the effectiveness of an ICT department by how quickly issues are resolved. When a service fails, most users are less concerned about the technical cause and more concerned about when they can continue with their work. Effective incident management ensures that every issue is reported, recorded, prioritized according to its impact, assigned to the right personnel, and monitored until it is resolved. This creates accountability and helps maintain user confidence in IT services.

What Goes Wrong When It Is Missing or Done Poorly

When incident management processes are not in place, confusion and delays are common. Staff may report the same issue multiple times without realizing that someone else has already reported it. There may be no clear responsibility for resolving the problem, causing delays in response and recovery. Critical issues can be treated as low-priority problems, resulting in prolonged service disruptions. Over time, users lose trust in the ICT department because they feel that their concerns are not being addressed effectively.


AWWDA Example

At AWWDA, an incident could occur when a staff member's computer crashes and important work files become inaccessible. If there is no proper backup and incident management process, valuable data may be lost, causing delays and affecting service delivery. To prevent this, the ICT department can regularly back up each staff member's data to an external hard drive or other secure backup storage. If a computer fails or files are accidentally deleted, the data can be quickly restored from the backup, minimizing disruption and ensuring that no important information is permanently lost. This approach helps maintain business continuity and protects organizational data.
