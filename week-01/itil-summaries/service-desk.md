 Service Desk
What It Is

The Service Desk is the central point of contact between staff and the ICT department for reporting incidents, requesting services, and seeking technical assistance. It acts as the communication bridge between users and ICT support teams, ensuring that all issues and requests are properly recorded, tracked, and resolved. The Service Desk is not only responsible for logging tickets but also for providing updates and ensuring a positive support experience for users.

Why It Matters

The Service Desk is often the first interaction staff members have with the ICT department. A well-organized Service Desk helps ensure that incidents and service requests are handled efficiently, users receive timely feedback, and no issue goes unnoticed. By centralizing all support activities, the ICT department can monitor workloads, identify recurring problems, measure performance, and improve service delivery. This helps build confidence and trust between staff and the ICT team.

What Goes Wrong When It Is Missing or Done Poorly

Without a Service Desk, staff may report issues directly to individual ICT personnel through phone calls, emails, or personal visits. This can result in some requests being forgotten, duplicated, or delayed because there is no central record of what has been reported. Workloads may become unevenly distributed, with some technicians overwhelmed while others remain unaware of pending issues. Additionally, management lacks visibility into the number of requests received, response times, and recurring issues, making it difficult to improve services

AWWDA Example

Currently, AWWDA does not have a dedicated Service Desk system. Staff members usually report ICT issues directly to available ICT personnel. While this may work for a small number of requests, it becomes difficult to track and manage issues as the organization grows. To improve service delivery, AWWDA could implement a centralized helpdesk system where staff submit incidents and service requests through an online portal or email. The system would automatically log requests, assign them to ICT staff, track their progress, and generate reports for management. This would improve accountability, response times, and overall ICT support services.
